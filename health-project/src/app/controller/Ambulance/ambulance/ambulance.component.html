Basic Life Support Ambulances

Equipped with essential medical gear and trained staff, these ambulances stabilize patients and offer basic care on-site, mainly for non-life-threatening situations, aiding in initial assessment and safe transportation.

Advanced Life Support Ambulances

Staffed by highly trained medical professionals and equipped with advanced gear, they administer medications, manage airways, monitor vital signs, and provide cardiac support for critical emergencies requiring immediate intervention.

Air Ambulances

Rapidly transport critically ill or injured patients over long distances using helicopters or planes, equipped with advanced medical gear and specialized teams, crucial in remote or accident-prone areas, reducing travel time to medical facilities.

E-Rickshaw Ambulances

Compact and agile, these vehicles equipped with basic medical gear navigate narrow urban areas swiftly, reaching congested or inaccessible locations efficiently, providing prompt medical aid and transportation to nearby medical facilities.

Dead Body Ambulances

Also known as mortuary vans, these vehicles transport deceased individuals with dignity, preserving and ensuring compliance with cultural and legal standards, facilitating respectful transportation to mortuaries or funeral homes.
